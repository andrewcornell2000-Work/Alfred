# Report 2: Competitive Landscape — AI-Powered Productised Services for Micro-SMBs (1–20 staff)

**Method:** 23 searches across pricing pages, review sites (Capterra/G2/GetApp), and app stores.

**TLDR:** The clearest exploitable gaps are (1) **compliance/safety documentation** (trades already pay $29–$97 per document out of grudge necessity, and incumbents lock them into 12-month $119+/mo contracts), (2) **staff training/onboarding**, where Trainual deliberately vacated the sub-25-employee market ($249/mo + $1,000 setup + 10-seat minimum), and (3) **voice-to-report field documentation**, where incumbents charge enterprise per-user pricing and the micro end is only just being attacked by tiny new apps. AI receptionists and generic forms are saturated; review management is well-served at the low end.

### Who they target
Trainual explicitly says it's "best suited for companies with 25–1000 employees" ([trainual.com/pricing](https://trainual.com/pricing)) and doubled revenue to $32.6M by moving upmarket. TalentLMS/iSpring target SMB-to-mid-market HR. SC Training and Connecteam target frontline/deskless teams but monetise at 10+ users. Nobody's paid product is designed for the 4-person cleaning company.

### Common complaints
- Capterra reviewers on Trainual: *"The cost is extremely expensive for small businesses"* and *"incredibly expensive… an ongoing expense that will forever need to be paid for the life of your business"* ([microgaps.com analysis](https://www.microgaps.com/gaps/lightweight-sop-training-platform)).
- The 10-seat floor means "a team of three still pays for ten"; first-year Core cost ≈ **$3,988** ([hackceleration.com review](https://hackceleration.com/labs/review/trainual)).
- *"The structure that helps a 40-person team is overhead a 10-person team will not finish building"* ([aistackscout.com](https://aistackscout.com/ai-onboarding-tools-new-hire-ramp-2026/)).
- The dominant "competitor" is Google Docs: *"small teams want structured SOPs with training tracking, but they cannot justify spending $100 to $250 per month"* ([microgaps.com](https://www.microgaps.com/gaps/small-team-sop-training-platform)).

### The gap
A **done-for-you onboarding manual**: owner does a 60–90 minute brain-dump call (or voice notes), AI turns it into a branded training site with quizzes and completion tracking for ≤20 staff. $999 setup replaces Trainual's $1,000 implementation fee but *includes the content creation Trainual makes the owner do themselves*; $50–150/mo hosting/updates undercuts every incumbent's floor. No integrations needed.

### Do micro-SMBs pay?
Mixed but real. Free tiers suppress pure-software willingness to pay. But high-turnover micro businesses demonstrably pay when it's tied to turnover pain — Trainual's average customer pays ~$340/mo ([microgaps.com](https://www.microgaps.com/gaps/lightweight-sop-training-platform)). The purchase trigger is a hiring event, which is predictable and easy to market to.

### Saturation: **3/5** (software saturated incl. free tiers; the done-for-you micro segment is genuinely open — incumbents structurally can't serve it at their price points).

| New micro AI apps | Speakwise **$59.99/yr**; TradieScribe **AU$69/user/mo**; SiteVoice.ai, BuildLog AI, Voice Log Pro (mostly 2025-founded) | [speakwiseapp.com](https://speakwiseapp.com/blog/best-app-foreman-daily-field-report), [tradiescribe.com.au](https://tradiescribe.com.au/), [sitevoice.ai](https://sitevoice.ai/) |

### Who they target
Raken/Fieldwire/SiteDocs: commercial GCs and mid-size contractors where the daily report is a contractual deliverable. CompanyCam: multi-crew home services (3-user minimum literally excludes solos). SafetyCulture: frontline ops teams, increasingly enterprise. The new voice apps target exactly the micro end — but they're immature and consumer-priced.

### Common complaints
- Raken on TrustRadius: *"The prior year cost me roughly $4,300… for the coming year the fees would have exceeded $17,000… I regret not cancelling"* ([trustradius.com](https://www.trustradius.com/compare-products/buildertrend-vs-raken)); Capterra reviewers flag surprise year-two price increases ([contractortoolstack.com](https://contractortoolstack.com/software/raken/)).
- CompanyCam: *"I'm a small company so paying around $100 a month…"* — the 3-user minimum forces solos to pay for unused seats ([roofingsoftwareguide.com](https://roofingsoftwareguide.com/reviews/companycam-review/)).
- SafetyCulture: "the simple act of doing an inspection gets buried under buttons you don't need"; steep learning curve ([fluix.io review](https://fluix.io/blog/safetyculture-review), [softwarefinder.com](https://softwarefinder.com/accounting-software/iauditor/reviews)).
- Sector-wide: "Most systems are either too complicated, too expensive, or just not built for how field teams actually work" ([constructionlogs.com](https://www.constructionlogs.com/post/best-construction-daily-report-software-2026-guide)); when reporting takes too long, workers simply stop filing ([wombat.software](https://www.wombat.software/blog/when-safety-becomes-extra-work-people-opt-out)).

### The gap
"**Talk for 2 minutes, client-ready branded PDF arrives**" — set up per business (their logo, their report sections, their client list), flat per-company pricing instead of per-user, no app training required. The done-for-you angle (you configure their templates and phone workflow in the $999 setup) is exactly what app-store products don't offer.

### Do micro-SMBs pay?
Yes when documentation is externally required (client reports, insurance, disputes, strata evidence). TradieScribe charging AU$69/user/mo shows the micro price point is being tested right now. Risk: Speakwise at $59.99/yr shows app-store anchoring can drag prices down — the defence is the productised setup + client-branded output.

### Saturation: **2.5/5** (enterprise end saturated; micro-AI end emerging fast but no dominant player, mostly solo-dev apps with no service layer).

- **Typeform**: Free (10 responses); Basic $25–29/mo for **100 responses**; Business $83–99/mo ([splitforms.com](https://splitforms.com/blog/typeform-vs-jotform))

- **ServiceM8 / Jobber** (trade intake bundled): ServiceM8 free–$149/mo; Jobber $29–199/mo ([getjobber.com/pricing](https://www.getjobber.com/pricing/))

### The gap
Generic forms are a race to zero (Tally's free unlimited plan destroyed the floor). The only defensible version is **vertical pre-job qualification**, but the vertical version is a feature, not a company, and FSM platforms are bundling it.

### Saturation: **5/5** generic / **3/5** vertical trade intake.

## Category 4: SOP / Process Documentation Generators

### Main players and actual pricing
- **Scribe**: Free (browser capture); Pro Personal $25–35/user/mo; Pro Team $13–17/seat/mo, **5-seat minimum** ([scribe.com/pricing](https://scribe.com/pricing))
- **Tango**: Free (limited); Pro $15–20/user/mo; Business $24/user/mo ([happysupport.ai](https://happysupport.ai/blog/scribe-vs-tango))
- **Whale**: Free (5 members); $99/mo (10 users, +$10/user); $249/mo ([usewhale.io/pricing](https://usewhale.io/pricing/))
- **SweetProcess**: $99/mo up to 10 users (+$5/user) ([sweetprocess.com](https://www.sweetprocess.com/sweetprocess-pricing/))
- **Process Street**: sales-gated; ~$100/mo Startup, ~$1,500/mo Pro estimated ([hackceleration.com](https://hackceleration.com/labs/review/process-street))

### The gap
Scribe/Tango's browser-capture mechanism is **useless for physical work** like cleaning or landscaping. Every tool assumes the *owner* will do the documenting — they don't, which is why Google Docs graveyards persist. AI-from-voice/video ("walk me through it on your phone, we produce the SOP pack") is a service, not software. Strong bundle with Category 1.

### Saturation: **4/5** software / **2/5** for done-for-you physical-work documentation.

## Category 8: Compliance / Safety Documentation (SWMS, toolbox talks, OSHA, food safety)

### Main players and actual pricing
- **HazardCo** (AU/NZ): tradie plans AU$119–199/mo + GST, builder plans AU$219–299/mo — **12-month lock-in** ([hazardco.com](https://www.hazardco.com/au/hazardco-pricing/))
- **SWMS Generator**: $29 one-off, 10 for $99, 50 for $199 — "No monthly subscriptions… Exactly how trades prefer it" ([swmsgenerator.com.au](https://www.swmsgenerator.com.au/plans_pricing))
- **OH Consultant**: $29 one-off; $19/mo solo unlimited; $59/mo business ([swms.ohconsultant.com.au](https://swms.ohconsultant.com.au/pricing))
- **BlueSafe**: one-off Word templates $44.95–96.80 with free branding ([bluesafeonline.com.au](https://www.bluesafeonline.com.au/))
- **US toolbox talks**: Safety Meeting App **$199–349/year flat** for 1–10 users ([safetymeetingapp.com](https://safetymeetingapp.com/our-pricing/)); Weeklysafety $25–39/mo; Make Safety Easy $349–1,199/mo flat tiers
- **Food safety**: FoodDocs $79–299/site/mo, AI HACCP in ~1 hour ([fooddocs.com/pricing](https://www.fooddocs.com/pricing)); Jolt/Operandio custom-priced

### The gap
**AI voice-driven, done-for-you compliance pack for 1–20-person trades and food businesses**: describe the job by voice → site-specific SWMS/JSA + matching toolbox talk + QR sign-on. $999 setup (custom safety policy pack, templates branded and pre-filled) + $50–150/mo for unlimited documents and a compliance calendar. It slots exactly between the $29 template shops (no service, no system) and HazardCo (contracted, expensive, still form-driven).

### Do micro-SMBs pay?
**The strongest signal in this research.** Sole traders pay $29–97 per document repeatedly because a principal contractor won't let them on site without it. US micro-contractors pay $199+/yr for toolbox-talk apps. Food venues pay $79+/mo because health inspections force it. This is grudge/compliance spend — it doesn't require selling ROI, only removing pain, which suits a founder who's bad at sales.

### Saturation: **2/5** (cheap template generators and expensive systems exist; the AI-voice + done-for-you + no-lock-in middle for micro businesses is thin).

# Ranked: Top 5 Exploitable Gaps for a Solo-Dev Productised AI Service

**1. Compliance/safety documentation (Category 8) — best overall fit.**
Payment is compliance-forced (no ROI selling needed — ideal for a weak salesperson), micro-trades already pay per-document, incumbents are either locked-in subscriptions or dumb templates, and nobody offers AI-voice-to-SWMS with done-for-you setup. Zero integrations; the buyer is the owner; $999 setup + $99/mo is priced *between* existing options rather than against them. Saturation 2/5. Main risk: jurisdiction-specific legal accuracy — mitigate by starting with one market and clear "review before use" framing.

**2. Staff training/onboarding for 1–20 staff (Category 1) — biggest documented vacuum.**
Trainual publicly abandoned this segment and Capterra quotes prove micro-owners want it but won't pay those prices. The $999 done-for-you setup directly replaces the part owners actually fail at — creating the content. Risks: free tiers anchor software prices, and churn once the manual is "done" — countered by tying the monthly fee to updates + new-hire onboarding events.

**3. Voice-to-report field documentation (Category 2) — strongest pain-to-simplicity ratio.**
The evening-paperwork pain is vivid and universal (cleaning, security, landscaping, care shift notes — not just construction), incumbents charge per-user enterprise pricing with documented "$4,300 → $17,000" horror stories, and the emerging AI competition is app-store-grade with no service layer. Risk: fast-moving space and $59/yr price anchors from consumer apps. Saturation 2.5/5, rising.

**4. SOP/process documentation done-for-you (Category 4) — best as a bundle, not standalone.**
Screen-capture tools structurally can't document physical work. As a companion offer to #2 ("we document your operations manual from your voice/video walkthroughs"), it strengthens the training offer and raises the $999 setup's perceived value.

**5. Quoting helpers for trades (Category 7) — proven wallet, but shrinking window.**
Trades demonstrably pay $29–150/mo, but Jobber/Tradify/ServiceM8 are bundling AI quoting into plans the target customer may already own. Only worth attacking as a hyper-specific wedge.

**Explicitly avoid:** AI receptionists (5/5 saturated, ~19% caller hang-up problem), generic forms/intake (Tally made the baseline free), review management (the low end is already served well at $29–75/mo).

**Cross-cutting observation:** categories 1, 2, 4, and 8 compound. A single positioning — "we turn what your business knows into the documents it needs: training manuals, SOPs, shift reports, and safety packs, from your voice" — lets one AI pipeline (voice/video in → structured branded documents out) serve four gaps, with compliance (the grudge purchase) as the easiest entry wedge and training/SOPs as the natural upsell.
