# Report 5: Compliance-Forced Verticals — Willingness to Pay (AU + US)

**Scope:** Which local business verticals are *forced* (by law, regulator, insurer, head contractor, franchisor, or client contract) to spend on documentation, training, reporting or compliance — and can pay a solo developer for a productised AI service (~$999 setup + monthly). AU + US, with AU-specific depth.
**Method:** 26 web searches across all 15 verticals.

---

## 1. NDIS providers & disability/aged-care support (AU)

**(a) Forced documentation.** The deepest regulator-forced documentation burden of any vertical studied. Registered providers must: notify the NDIS Commission of reportable incidents **within 24 hours** of awareness, with a full written report in 5 business days ([NDIS Commission fact sheet](https://www.ndiscommission.gov.au/sites/default/files/2022-02/factsheet-incident-reporting-management-and-prevention.pdf), [detailed guidance](https://www.ndiscommission.gov.au/sites/default/files/2024-10/detailed-guidance-reportable-incidents.pdf)); maintain a full incident management system recording *all* incidents; keep progress notes that substantiate every claim for payment — auditors demand "credible, real-time evidence," and copy-paste or vague notes are explicit audit red flags ([Appoyo audit guide](https://www.appoyo.com.au/blog/ndis-progress-notes-audit-and-case-note-guide), [ShiftCare audit-readiness](https://shiftcare.com/blog/ndis-audit-readiness-what-auditors-look-for-how-prepare)). Registration requires third-party audits: verification $900–$1,500, certification $3,000–$10,000+, full registration cycle $15,000–$25,000+ ([LMS Compliance](https://lmscomplianceservices.com.au/blog/ndis-internal-audit-guide/)). Poor progress notes and weak incident management are the most common audit failures.

**(b) Size/budget.** 12,555 registered providers averaging ~$1M in half-year payments each, plus 133,348 unregistered providers averaging ~$34,800 ([NDIA Annual Pricing Review 2026–27](https://www.ndis.gov.au/media/8701/download?attachment=)). A registered provider with $1M+ revenue facing $10k–25k audit cycles easily absorbs $999 + $200–500/mo.

**(c) Adjacent spend.** ShiftCare $9–25/user/mo (5-user min); Brevity ~$3.99–6.49/client/mo ([ShiftCare pricing](https://shiftcare.com/pricing), [Brevity pricing](https://www.brevity.com.au/pricing/)). Compliance consultants routinely sell registration/audit-prep engagements.

**(d) Reachability.** Exceptional for a non-salesperson: ndipreneur runs a ~14,000-member Facebook group and a 20,000+ professional community with expos in every capital ([ndipreneur events](https://events.ndipreneur.au/)); Whole Warrior Network runs state-based provider Facebook groups ([wholewarriornetwork.com.au](https://www.wholewarriornetwork.com.au/)). Providers actively ask for compliance help in these groups.

**(e) Turnover.** Support-worker churn is high (~20–30%+); every new worker must produce compliant notes from day one.

**(f) AI adoption.** Validated but crowding fast at the worker-note layer: KoalaOne, NoteScribe ($49/mo), NDISNotes, Clio Care, Plannor all sell AI voice-to-progress-notes. The less-contested layer is the *provider-level* audit stack: incident triage with 24-hour reportable logic, training registers, one-click audit evidence packs.

## 2. Construction subcontractors — SWMS (AU) & OSHA records (US)

Every subcontractor PCBU doing high-risk construction work must prepare its own site-specific SWMS *before* work starts; principal contractors must review each sub's SWMS before allowing work ([SafeWork NSW](https://www.safework.nsw.gov.au/your-industry/construction/construction/general-requirements/prepare-safe-work-method-statement)). NSW inspectors issue **on-the-spot penalties of $3,600 (individual) / $18,000 (body corporate)** for SWMS offences ([OH Consultant](https://swms.ohconsultant.com.au/swms-sole-trader)). SafeWork NSW estimates **60%+ of SWMS reviewed on site are inadequate**. In the US: OSHA 300/300A/301 logs, plus client-mandated prequalification platforms: ISNetworld from $875/yr, Avetta $450–900/yr, small subs paying $5k+/yr across networks plus ~120 hours writing safety programs ([ExpiryEdge](https://expiryedge.com/articles/avetta-vs-isnetworld-comparison/)).

Adjacent spend: SWMS templates $29–80/doc; SWMS SaaS $15–59/mo. 74% of US contractors see AI as key to efficiency but only 12% have embedded it ([CoterieLabs](https://coterielabs.com.au/field-notes/trades-ai-adoption-gap-training-barrier-2026)). AI SWMS generators already exist (OneClickSWMS) — validating demand but occupying the obvious wedge. Cheap template anchors make a $999 setup harder to justify for SWMS alone.

## 3. Commercial cleaning companies

One of the most compliance-dense SMB sectors in AU: Cleaning Services Award records (7-year retention), SDS for every chemical, documented chemical-handling training, site inductions, incident reports, SWMS demanded in tenders ([Reguladar](https://reguladar.com.au/blog/commercial-cleaning-compliance)). The forcing function is *contractual*: facility managers and government tenders require insurance certificates, WHS policy, sample SWMS and induction evidence before site access ([CAF Standard](https://www.cleaningaccountability.org.au/certification/caf-standard/)).

45,014 businesses in AU ([IBISWorld](https://www.ibisworld.com/australia/number-of-businesses/commercial-cleaning-services/574/)); ~1M janitorial businesses in the US. Adjacent spend: Swept $30–225/mo; Janitorial Manager ~$500/mo. Reachability good: ISSA (5,800 member orgs), BSCAI, big owner Facebook groups. **Turnover extreme — ~200% annually**; owners rehire their entire workforce twice a year ([Level benchmarks](https://levelcfo.com/benchmarks/cleaning/)). AI adoption low; existing tools are scheduling/timekeeping, not document generation.

## 4. Security guard companies

Statutory, not just contractual: Master licensees must keep **incident registers** recorded before the involved guard's shift ends, in approved form, retained 3 years, open to police inspection ([SPAAL fact sheet](https://spaal.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2025/06/SEC_-_Fact_Sheet_2_-_Incident_Registers_2023.pdf)); every guard needs state licences backed by RTO training ([ASIAL](https://asial.com.au/Web/Web/Advice-Services/Security-Licensing.aspx)). Clients contractually demand daily activity reports — what guard companies get judged and rebid on.

~7,700–8,000 US contract security firms in a $49.1B industry. Adjacent spend: Silvertrac from **$249/mo + $842–1,404 one-time setup** on 12-month contracts — an almost perfect price anchor for a $999-setup productised service. **Turnover extreme: 100–300% annually at many firms; sector average 77%** ([DSP analysis](https://www.dronestrategicpartners.com/post/the-security-guard-shortage-why-turnover-is-accelerating-the-shift-to-automation)). Incumbents are form-builders, not AI writers. Whitespace for "guard speaks, client-ready branded DAR/incident report comes out."

## 5. Electrical / plumbing / HVAC trades

AU: certificates of compliance for fixed electrical work (CCEW lodged via mandatory portal within 7 days; $1,000 on-the-spot fines), test-and-tag records, SWMS. The best-funded SMB trades: ServiceTitan $245–500/technician/mo; a 5-tech shop pays $1,200–2,000/mo on software ([Pipeline On](https://pipelineon.com/blog/servicetitan-pricing/)). But compliance certificates are structured portal lodgements — less AI-solvable from voice/photos than narrative documents. Heavily marketed-to.

## 6. Childcare centres

NQF documented programs, assessments, incident records — but ACECQA is actively campaigning to *reduce* over-documentation ([ACECQA QA1](https://www.acecqa.gov.au/qa1-educational-program-documentation-educators-and-teachers-early-childhood)), which weakens a compliance-fear pitch. Centres pay Brightwheel ~$200–400/mo; incumbents are already adding AI observation drafting. Turnover high (17–32%). Solo-dev whitespace narrow.

## 7. Restaurants / cafés — food safety logging

AU Standard 3.2.2A: certified Food Safety Supervisor + records substantiating temperature control, retained ≥3 months ([FSANZ](https://www.foodstandards.gov.au/sites/default/files/2023-12/Standard%203.2.2A%20Food%20Safety%20Management%20Tools-%20corrected%20271123.pdf)). But the job is checklists and temperature probes — sensors beat LLMs; incumbents (FoodDocs $99–299/site/mo, Squizify from $69/mo) already cheap and good. Turnover 65–75%. **Weak fit for language-AI.**

## 8. Independent gyms / fitness studios

Mostly duty-of-care + voluntary code; no regulator audits documentation routinely → weak forcing function. **Not recommended.**

## 9. Removalists / movers

US interstate movers face federal document mandates (49 CFR Part 375), but local movers are lightly regulated; AU AFRA accreditation is voluntary. **Not recommended.**

## 10. Landscaping / lawn care

Forced documentation applies only to chemical applicators. Compliance slice too narrow. **Not recommended standalone.**

## 11. Pest control

Genuinely statutory: every pesticide application recorded within 48 hours (NSW), kept 3 years ([NSW EPA](https://www.epa.nsw.gov.au/Your-environment/Pesticides/compulsory-record-keeping)). But GorillaDesk $49–149/route/mo already bundles chemical tracking cheaply — the AI wedge is thin. Just misses the top 8.

## 12. Real estate property management (AU)

Strong forced documentation: annual trust account audits, monthly reconciliations, 7-year records, evidenced routine inspections ([PMVA checklist](https://www.pmva.com.au/property-management-compliance-checklist/)). Agencies pay PropertyMe $149–215/mo per 100 properties. But incumbents are already shipping AI (PropertyMe "AiMe Comply", Inspection Manager with 20,000+ PMs) — whitespace shrinking.

## 13. Medical / dental / allied health private practices

Highest ability to pay of all verticals, and AI documentation is *proven*: Heidi cut physio documentation from 70→36 min/day with 86% median voluntary adoption ([Heidi impact report](https://www.talkinghealthtech.com/news/fresh-insights-into-heidi%E2%80%99s-impact)) — but that proof means the scribe layer is saturated (Heidi, Lyrebird at freemium). Only practice-level accreditation-evidence automation remains open.

## 14. Mobile car detailing

Lowest compliance burden studied; tiny budgets. **Eliminate.**

## 15. Franchise systems

Franchisors supply and mandate the stack (franchise LMSs, contractual field audits). *Don't sell to franchisees* (the franchisor controls their tools); selling **to franchisors** is a real but enterprise-cycle play misaligned with a $999 productised motion. Useful as a channel later (one franchisor deal = N locations), not as the entry vertical.

## Best vertical-problem pairing

**Small registered NDIS providers (roughly 5–50 support workers) × the incident-to-audit evidence chain** — voice-first incident capture with built-in 24-hour reportable-incident logic, auto-drafted Commission notifications, linked progress notes, and a one-click audit evidence pack.

Why this beats everything else:
1. **The spend is legally unavoidable and priced in thousands, not tens.** A missed 24-hour notification is an enforcement matter; weak incident management is the single most common audit failure; providers already pay $3k–25k per audit cycle plus consultants. Against that, $999 setup + ~$299/mo reads as cheap insurance.
2. **Reachability requires zero cold-sales skill.** The only vertical studied where 20,000+ owners self-congregate in communities that explicitly welcome compliance content.
3. **Inputs are exactly voice/docs/photos, no integrations.** Heidi's 86% voluntary adoption in adjacent care settings proves frontline workers will use voice documentation.
4. **The positioning gap is real.** Crowded entrants sell $15–50/mo *worker note-writers*. Nobody owns the **provider-level "pass your audit" productised service**.

**Runner-up** (if the founder wants a globally sellable product from day one): **security guard companies × voice-to-DAR/incident reports** — Silvertrac's $249/mo + ~$1k setup pricing proves the exact business model, turnover up to 300% guarantees the training pain never goes away, and incumbents are form-builders, not AI writers.

*Note: both pairings were subjected to dedicated GO/NO-GO validation dives — see reports 07 and 08.*
