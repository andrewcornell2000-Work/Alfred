# Report 4: Selling Productised AI Services to SMBs — Models, Case Studies, Pricing

**For:** a solo developer, weak at sales, targeting ~$999 setup + $50–300/month.
**Method:** 20 web searches; sources include Indie Hackers, GoHighLevel published case studies, MicroConf talks, agency benchmark reports, reseller platform docs, and practitioner blogs. Vendor-published numbers are flagged.

---

## (a) Case studies with numbers

### 1. AR Funnel (Mike Crowson) — GHL white-label for fitness coaches, $2.5M recurring
- **Sells:** white-labelled GoHighLevel + done-for-you onboarding + group coaching. **Pricing: $1,000 upfront + $497/month, no contracts.**
- **Revenue:** $0 → $200K+ MRR in 3 years, 350 clients. Churn peaked at 24%; they **paused acquisition for 8 months** to rebuild onboarding, got churn to single digits. Key ratio: **80% template / 20% custom**.
- URL: https://www.gohighlevel.com/case-studies/ar-funnel *(vendor-published; treat as best-case)*

### 2. LeadEngage (Wade Lester) — niche middleware SaaS on GHL, $375K/yr, zero ad spend
- **Sells:** HighLevel texting/automation bridged into Follow Up Boss (real-estate CRM). **Tiers $149 / $197 / $297/month.**
- **Revenue:** yr1 $62K → yr2 $172K → yr3 $375K gross, ~300 customers.
- **Sales:** **entirely organic — answering questions in the Follow Up Boss and HighLevel Facebook communities + word of mouth.** This is the single best "bad at sales" template in the dataset: pick a community that already congregates around a tool/problem, solve its gap.
- URL: https://www.gohighlevel.com/case-studies/leadengage *(vendor-published)*

### 3. AdsExpress (Jose Graxirena) — GHL SaaS mode, $116K/yr via daily YouTube webinars
- **Tiers $107–$397/month + setup fees.** 115 recurring clients, zero ad spend. Inbound, but very content-labour-intensive.
- URL: https://www.gohighlevel.com/case-studies/adsexpress *(vendor-published)*

### 4. "Growth CRM" (Vida Digital) — white-label CRM saved a dying agency
- **Numbers:** $8,000/month recurring, 157 users at $55/month, ~78% margin; client retention went **67% → 94%** and monthly churn 15% → 6% after rebranding GHL as "their own" CRM.
- **Lesson:** the *perception* of proprietary software dramatically improves retention vs. reselling visible third-party tools.
- URL: https://vidadigitalsolutions.com/blog/agency-lost-clients-saas-mode-gohighlevel-saved-business

### 5. Jeremy DuPont — sold white-label AI receptionists to 200+ of his existing 350 clients in ~4–5 months
- **$2K setup fee** (waived for a 20-clinic beta) + monthly recurring. Physical-therapy clinics.
- **Sales:** a scarcity/co-creation script ("I'm taking on 10 people to help shape this") sent to his **existing client book** — the highest-converting channel that exists.
- URL: https://scale-an-agency.captivate.fm/episode/how-to-sell-ai-receptionists-to-200-clients-the-white-label-playbook-retell-versai

### 6. Lis Bueno — $22K AI voice agent + $1,500/month retainer, via a partner who could sell
- She built; her partner navigated procurement and won a 25-bidder process. Textbook "builder pairs with seller" division of labour.
- URL: https://www.stork.ai/blog/22k-ai-deal-zero-code-needed

### 7. WP Curve (Dan Norris) — the canonical inbound productised service, $1M ARR in ~18 months
- **Sells:** unlimited small WordPress fixes, **$59–79/month flat**, launched in 7 days.
- **Revenue:** 10 customers week one → $33K MRR at month 13 → 700 customers, sold to GoDaddy. **Zero paid ads: 250 blog posts in year one.**
- **Lesson:** ruthless "no" to anything outside the core offer is what made low-touch selling possible.
- URLs: https://beanninjas.com/blog/dan-norris/ · https://saasclub.io/podcast/dan-norris-wpcurve/

### 8. Designjoy (Brett Williams) — $1.5M ARR solo, literally zero sales calls

## GoHighLevel white-label analysis

**Why it works for non-technical sellers.** (1) Fixed-cost leverage: $497/month flat regardless of client count — 50 clients ≈ **$12.8K net MRR at 87% margin**. (2) SaaS Mode automates provisioning, billing, rebilling. (3) **Snapshots** turn delivery into copy-paste: a niche configuration built once, deployed per client in hours, and sellable to other agencies for $97–$497. (4) The sale is carried by three instantly-demonstrable features — **missed-call text-back, review requests, database reactivation** — each with ROI a plumber can compute on a napkin. (5) "Your own branded software" positioning measurably lifts retention (67%→94% in the Vida Digital case).

**Weaknesses.** Platform bloated for local owners ("the other 80% of the platform is where local owners get distracted and cancel" — https://nestway.ai/gohighlevel-for-local-businesses); 2–4 week learning curve; all tier-1 support lands on the reseller; usage-fee bill shock; and **thousands of resellers sell the identical product** — differentiation collapses to niche and service.

**Lessons for a developer who can build:**
1. GHL proves demand and price point ($297/month is a validated number local SMBs pay); the winning asset is **niche + templated config + onboarding system**, not software breadth.
2. SMBs only use ~3 features. A developer can build just those — better margins, no $497 tax, no platform bloat driving churn.
3. Or invert the model: **be the platform, not the reseller** — recruit commission-motivated sellers instead of learning sales.
4. Copy GHL's *retention* mechanics regardless: white-label branding, month-to-month, snapshot-style templated delivery.

## Pricing psychology at $999 setup + monthly

**What makes owners say yes fast:**
- **Loss framing beats feature framing.** "6 missed calls/day × $250 jobs = ~$15K/month leaking" — owners buy *recovered revenue*, not AI (https://www.strategyeye.com/the-untapped-goldmine-in-missed-business-calls/).
- **Personalised demo = the close.** "The conversation shifts from 'should I buy this?' to 'when can we go live?'" (https://pickaxe.co/post/sell-ai-agents-to-local-businesses).
- **Risk reversal that costs nothing.** 14-day money-back guarantee — "nobody ever asks, but it kills objections" (https://blog.tixu.ai/earn-300-client-selling-ai-to-local-businesses/).
- **Paid pilots, never free.** Paid pilots convert 60–90%; free pilots get deprioritised; credit the pilot fee toward setup (https://angkan.life/should-your-saas-pilot-be-free/).
- **Anchoring & tiering:** present a $2,500 "full" tier so $999 reads as the sensible middle.

**Churn patterns:** retainer-model services average **18% annual churn (56-month client lifespan)** vs 42% for project-based; **~43% of churn happens in the first 90 days**, making onboarding the #1 retention lever (https://focus-digital.co/average-marketing-agency-churn/ ; https://forge.so/agency-benchmarks). Also structural: local businesses die (~half of trades within 5 years) — screen for 2+ years in business.

**Pricing recommendation:**
- **Setup: keep $999 but position against a $2,500 anchor tier.** Market rates for comparable done-for-you AI builds are $1,500–$4,000. Waive it for the first 5–10 in exchange for testimonials/case-study rights, then charge everyone after.
- **Monthly: aim at $199–$297, not $50–$150.** $297/month is the most validated local-SMB number in this research; the low end barely covers support-minutes and attracts the neediest clients. Hard cost will be $20–100/client (80%+ margin).
- **Structure:** month-to-month; 14-day money-back; pilot = $250–500, credited to setup on conversion; a "one recovered job pays for it" ROI line on every invoice.
- **Ladder:** entry offer ($199/month) → core ($999 setup at $297/month) → expansion modules ($99–199/month each). Expansion revenue against the existing base is far easier than new-logo sales.

## Delivery models that minimise support burden

1. **80/20 templating (AR Funnel ratio):** one master template per vertical; every client is configuration, not construction.
2. **No-call, intake-form onboarding:** published fixed scope + Typeform/Tally intake replaces discovery calls; Designjoy runs $1.5M ARR with zero meetings.
3. **White-glove-then-automate:** hand-hold the first 90 days (where 43% of churn lives), then shift to automated weekly value reports — "here are the 14 reports your AI produced." The report *is* the retention mechanism.
4. **Self-serve content updates:** constrained dashboard for the things clients change often so they never email you.
5. **SOP-and-automation backbone:** record every delivery step once as a Loom, convert to SOP with AI, automate onboarding triggers.
6. **Design for failure before launch:** explicit human-handoff triggers, monitoring loop, honest scope on what the AI won't handle.

## Top 5 lessons

1. **The sales problem is a positioning problem first.** Every low-sales-skill success sold a *pre-understood outcome* ("never miss a call," "unlimited fixes for $69") in a *tight niche* — never "AI services." When the offer is obvious, the checkout page does the selling.
2. **Pick a channel where trust pre-exists: a niche community or a partner's client book.** The two most sales-averse-friendly wins — LeadEngage and DuPont — involved essentially zero persuasion. The fastest version: build the product and let an agency/partner with an existing SMB book sell it for a revenue share.
3. **Let the demo do the selling.** Building a working artifact from the prospect's own material before you ever talk to them is the one outbound motion where *engineering skill substitutes for sales skill*.
4. **GHL's real invention is the snapshot + $297/month price point + automated billing.** Copy the economics; build the focused 20% SMBs actually use — or flip sides and sell your platform through resellers who love selling.
5. **Retention is won in the first 90 days and by conversation design, not features.** Budget as much effort for onboarding, monitoring dashboards, and human-handoff paths as for the build — single-digit churn is what turns 20 clients at $297 into a durable ~$70K/year business rather than a treadmill.
