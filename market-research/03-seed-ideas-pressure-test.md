# Report 3: Pressure-Test of the 3 Original Seed Ideas

**Method:** 20 searches across pricing pages, review aggregators, industry benchmarks, operator forums, and competitor landscapes.

**TL;DR:** Idea 2 (voice-to-report) is the only one where demand is *mandated* rather than merely nice-to-have, where the exact pricing model proposed ($999 setup + monthly) is already being paid, and where the "zero-integration" constraint is an advantage rather than a liability. Idea 1 is salvageable only if repositioned as a done-for-you service, and its recurring revenue is fragile. Idea 3 should be killed: the pain is real but is already solved for free inside the software the targets already own.

---

## IDEA 1 — Custom AI Staff Training Platform (Productised)

### Demand evidence
Mixed — the pain is real, but the evidence points to a *service* business, not a *product* business.

- Small businesses in the target verticals do buy training tools. A commercial cleaning company (Serene Clean) describes building a 6-hour orientation program on **Trainual** on a cleaning-industry podcast ([Podbean](https://www.podbean.com/media/share/pb-yh99b-1a53786?download=1)); Trainual's case studies feature an HVAC/plumbing firm cutting onboarding time 80% ([dupple.com/tools/trainual](https://dupple.com/tools/trainual)).
- The underlying pain is enormous and quantified: janitorial staff turnover runs **~200% annually (range 100–400%)** with direct recruit-and-train costs of **$2,300–$5,500 per hire** ([levelcfo.com](https://levelcfo.com/benchmarks/cleaning/), [employjoy.ai](https://www.employjoy.ai/blogs/the-15-2-billion-turnover-problem), [cleanlink.com](https://www.cleanlink.com/site/brand-feature/Combating-the-Industry-Turnover-Rate--55971)). Hospitality runs 70–80%. Training is *not* a one-time onboarding cost in these verticals — turnover makes it recurring. That's the strongest fact in this idea's favour.
- **The garbage-in problem is confirmed and severe.** Roughly 45% of small businesses have no formal SOPs at all ([theaitrades.ai](https://theaitrades.ai/blog/how-to-build-sops-for-home-service-business)); "it's all in my head" is *the* most common delegation barrier for trades and cleaning owners ([prosulum.com](https://www.prosulum.com/sops/), [tradebrain](https://os.tradebrain.ca/blog/what-is-an-sop-small-business-guide-for-trades-owners)). This kills a self-serve product but is precisely what a productised "we extract and build it for you" offer solves — consultancies already sell exactly this extraction service ([insidesmallbusiness.com.au](https://insidesmallbusiness.com.au/latest-news/the-owner-trap-why-founders-need-to-document-their-ip)).

### Competitors and pricing

| Competitor | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Trainual | ~$249–300/mo (10 seats) + **mandatory $1,000 implementation fee** | Explicitly no longer targets teams under ~15–25 ([trainual.com/pricing](https://trainual.com/pricing)) |
| Waybook | $49/mo (Create), $99/mo (Core, 20 members) | ([help.waybook.com](https://help.waybook.com/en/articles/8439802-waybook-plans)) |
| SC Training (EdApp) | **FREE up to 10 learners**, then $5/learner/mo; free pre-made cleaning/hospitality course libraries | ([training.safetyculture.com/pricing](https://training.safetyculture.com/pricing/)) |
| SweetProcess / Process Street / Whale | $85–100/mo | ([sweetprocess.com](https://www.sweetprocess.com/standard-operating-procedure-software/)) |
| Swept (cleaning vertical) | $30–225/mo, includes per-location cleaning instructions & checklists | ([crewski.com/reviews/swept](https://crewski.com/reviews/swept)) |
| Franchises (Jan-Pro, Jani-King) | Training bundled in franchise fee ($13k–30k) | The franchise segment is closed |

Note the pincer: SC Training is **free for exactly the target size (≤10 learners)** with pre-built commercial-cleaning courses, while Trainual's retreat upmarket confirms that selling software subscriptions to sub-25-person companies didn't work even for a well-funded company.

### Why they don't buy / churn
SMB training software is the canonical **shelfware** category: tools get bought, staff revert to old habits, owners cancel ([todaysmagazine.co.uk](https://todaysmagazine.co.uk/why-small-business-owners-are-prioritizing-staff-training-over-new-software/), [zylo.com](https://zylo.com/blog/shelfware)). Trainual setup realistically takes 10–15 hours of the owner's time ([atherobase.com](https://atherobase.com/review/trainual-review)). Once the content is built and current staff trained, the owner asks: "why am I still paying monthly?" The recurring fee is a hosting fee for static content — the weakest kind of recurring revenue.

### Realistic price tolerance
The **$999 setup fee is directly validated** — Trainual charges a mandatory $1,000 implementation fee. Monthly: $49–150/mo is the ceiling for 5–20 person firms; $250+ is proven to fail at this size.

### Hardest part of selling it
Convincing an owner who has *never written anything down* that documentation is worth $1,000 — selling against inertia, not against a competitor. Requires a consultative, workshop-style sale, which is exactly what a sales-averse founder finds hardest. Delivery itself is labour: every client is hours of content transformation — service margin, not software margin.

### Failure modes
1. Client pays setup, trains current staff, cancels the monthly within 6 months.
2. You become an SOP-writing agency with a portal attached — non-scalable hours per client.
3. SC Training's free tier or a Google Drive folder is "good enough" ([cleaningbusinessacademy.com](https://www.cleaningbusinessacademy.com/how-to-train-new-cleaners-without-babysitting-every-job/)).
4. Owner never supplies the source material (garbage-in), project stalls, refund dispute.

### Verdict: **VIABLE WITH CHANGES**
Only works as a **done-for-you content service with hosting attached**, sold on the turnover/retraining cost math. Price the recurring fee as "training updates + new-hire tracking" rather than hosting. Do not build a self-serve platform.

| Dimension | Score |
|---|---|
| Demand evidence | 6/10 |
| Willingness to pay | 5/10 |
| Ease of sale (sales-averse founder) | 3/10 |
| Recurring-revenue durability | 4/10 |

## IDEA 2 — Voice-to-Shift / End-of-Day Reporting

### Demand evidence
Strong — the only idea of the three where reporting is **contractually mandated**, not optional.

- In security, Daily Activity Reports are "not optional — a core requirement… if something is not documented, from a liability and legal standpoint, it effectively did not happen." Required by client contracts, used by attorneys and insurers ([pgss1.com](https://pgss1.com/2025/12/28/security-guard-daily-activity-report/), [allnationsecurity.com](https://allnationsecurity.com/the-importance-of-completing-daily-activity-reports-dars-for-security-guards/)). DAR quality directly affects contract renewal ([miratag.com](https://miratag.com/en/blog/security-daily-activity-report-client-reports)).
- In construction, daily logs are evidence in payment disputes, lien claims, and delay claims — Voice Log Pro markets itself as "defending against payment disputes" ([voicelogpro.com](https://www.voicelogpro.com/)).
- Sophisticated clients now *reject* paper: "If your plan says 'we'll use paper logs and phone check-ins,' sophisticated clients will pass" ([novagems.com](https://novagems.com/how-to-start-a-security-guard-company/)). Reporting is a **sales weapon** for the business owner — Swept customers "send monthly reports as a retention tool… a huge advantage when bidding new accounts" ([sweptworks.com](https://sweptworks.com/janitorial-inspection-software)).
- **Worker adoption of voice is specifically validated.** The #1 documented reason field crews abandon reporting apps is typing on phones; "voice input turns a 15-minute typing session into a 3-minute verbal summary… Demo the voice feature first. Always." ([buildlogapp.com](https://buildlogapp.com/blog/how-to-switch-paper-to-digital-daily-logs.html), [remato.com](https://remato.com/blog/mobile-first-construction-software-adoption/), [the-owners-playbook.com](https://the-owners-playbook.com/articles/why-your-foreman-wont-use-the-ipad)). Workers already send voice notes on WhatsApp — startups like Velora and OnSite (raised US$1.3M) exist purely to structure that behaviour ([velora.ai](https://velora.ai/blog/why-construction-apps-fail-on-site/), [digitalnewsasia.com](https://www.digitalnewsasia.com/startups/singapores-onsite-raises-us13-mil-streamline-construction-communication-ai)).

### Competitors and pricing

| Competitor | Price | Segment |
|---|---|---|
| Silvertrac | $249/mo (10 devices) + **$842–1,404 mandatory setup fee**, 12-mo contract | Mid-size security ([therms.io comparison](https://www.therms.io/blog/therms-vs-silvertrac-complete-comparison-guide-for-security-guard-management-software/)) |
| THERMS | $45/mo (5 users), no setup fee | SMB security |
| Trackforce Valiant | ~$8–15/guard/mo, median $14,400/yr for 50–200 guards | Mid/large ([zendikt.com](https://www.zendikt.com/product/trackforce-valiant)) |
| GuardMetrics | Consultative, month-to-month ([guardmetrics.com](https://guardmetrics.com/affordable-guard-management-system/)) | SMB security |
| Raken | ~$15–46/user/mo, year-two price-hike complaints | Construction |
| FieldSift | **$99/mo flat**, SMS/voice-in, no app | Construction subs ([fieldsift.com](https://fieldsift.com/)) |
| SiteVoice.ai, Voice Log Pro, OnSite, Benetics | Early-stage, mostly construction | Direct voice-to-report clones |
| Swept | $150–225/mo per company | Cleaning inspection/reporting |

### Why they don't buy / churn
- SMB security firms complain incumbents are too expensive; all-in-one suites eventually displace point solutions — reporting alone is a feature, not a platform.
- If the *client* of your customer stops demanding reports, the subscription is first on the chopping block.
- Guards/cleaners on personal phones may resist any employer app; SMS/WhatsApp-based capture (FieldSift's model) sidesteps this and is the right pattern.

### Realistic price tolerance
The standout finding: **the exact pricing model already exists in this market.** Silvertrac charges $842–1,404 mandatory setup plus $249/mo. $999 setup + ~$99–199/mo undercuts the incumbent while beating WhatsApp chaos. The defensible band for 5–20 person operations is $79–249/mo flat (not per-user — FieldSift explicitly wins by rejecting per-user pricing).

### Failure modes
1. Construction gets crowded out by funded startups; you survive only in the neglected verticals (security SMBs, cleaning, removalists) — plan for that from day one.
2. WhatsApp/ChatGPT DIY. Counter: branded, timestamped, archived, client-facing PDFs with an audit trail — the *evidentiary* packaging, not the transcript.
3. Incumbent suites add voice input as a checkbox feature (Raken already supports dictation).
4. Worker sends garbage voice notes → owner blames the tool. Mitigate with AI follow-up prompts ("you didn't mention headcount").

### Verdict: **STRONG** (the best of the three)
Mandated, recurring, per-shift demand; validated setup-fee pricing; voice modality is the documented cure for the adoption problem; zero-integration is genuinely viable here. Sell where reporting is compulsory (security first), not where it's optional.

| Dimension | Score |
|---|---|
| Demand evidence | 8/10 |
| Willingness to pay | 7/10 |
| Ease of sale | 6/10 — "your contracts already require this; here's a cheaper way to comply" is the easiest pitch of the three |
| Recurring-revenue durability | 7/10 — reports are needed every shift, forever |

## IDEA 3 — Smart Customer Intake for Trades

### Demand evidence
The pain is real and quantified — but the market has already priced it at $0.

- Wasted-time evidence is solid: ~40% of home-service sales time goes to leads that never convert ([pipelineon.com](https://pipelineon.com/blog/qualifying-leads-before-phone/)); a proper intake form saves 3–5 hours/week of wasted dispatch ([ivyforms.com](https://ivyforms.com/blog/lead-generation-for-plumbers/)).
- **But the solution is already free inside the tools trades own.** Jobber's request forms include customisable questions and photo upload out of the box ([help.getjobber.com](https://help.getjobber.com/hc/en-us/articles/115009737048-Request-Basics)); ServiceM8's online booking form with photo attachments is a **free add-on** ([servicem8.com](https://www.servicem8.com/features-online-booking)).
- Where trades *do* spend real money on intake, it's on the **phone**: Rosie $49/mo, Goodcall $79–99/mo, Sameday AI $449/mo, Hatch $500–2,000/mo, Avoca $1,000–5,000/mo ([blog.voc.ai](https://blog.voc.ai/best-ai-receptionist-for-hvac/)). Trades leads arrive by phone; a "send the customer a link" product fights the channel.
- Intaker's $80/mo works because it's **legal**, where one signed client is worth five figures. Trades economics don't support the same maths per lead.
- Generic AI conversational forms are already commoditised: Formless from $19–59/mo ([aiquiks.com](https://aiquiks.com/ai-tools/formless-by-typeform)), BuildFolio at $39/mo.

### Why they don't buy / churn
The office already has a "good enough" free form; leads arrive via phone, Google LSA, and Facebook where a link-based flow doesn't fit; value attribution is nearly impossible (an owner can't see which wasted visits *didn't* happen). SMBs cancel services whose value is invisible — the same dynamic that gives website-maintenance agencies 8–12% *monthly* churn ([gatilab.com](https://gatilab.com/sell-wordpress-maintenance-plans/)).

### Realistic price tolerance
$29–79/mo. A $999 setup fee for what looks like "a smart form" will be compared to free Jobber forms in the first minute of every sales call.

### Verdict: **WEAK — kill it**
Real pain, but already solved free at the point of need, sold into the wrong channel (links vs phone), no mandate, no price umbrella, hardest differentiation story of the three.

| Dimension | Score |
|---|---|
| Demand evidence | 5/10 |
| Willingness to pay | 3/10 |
| Ease of sale | 2/10 |
| Recurring-revenue durability | 3/10 |

## Final comparison

| | Idea 1: Training Platform | Idea 2: Voice-to-Report | Idea 3: Smart Intake |
|---|---|---|---|
| Verdict | VIABLE WITH CHANGES | **STRONG** | WEAK |
| Demand evidence | 6 | 8 | 5 |
| Willingness to pay | 5 | 7 | 3 |
| Ease of sale | 3 | 6 | 2 |
| Recurring durability | 4 | 7 | 3 |
| **Total** | **18/40** | **28/40** | **13/40** |

**Recommendation:** Pursue Idea 2, starting with small security companies (5–20 guards) — reporting is contractually mandated, incumbents charge $249/mo plus four-figure setup fees, and cheap alternatives still require typing. The wedge: guard sends a voice note, the owner's client receives a branded, timestamped DAR PDF — no app installs, no integration. Keep Idea 1 in reserve as a cross-sell to the same customer base (security and cleaning both have extreme turnover). Kill Idea 3.

One caution that applies to whichever is picked: SMB recurring revenue is fragile everywhere — the documented cure is making value visible monthly (automated summary emails showing reports generated, hours saved), because SMBs reliably cancel services they can't see working ([gatilab.com](https://gatilab.com/sell-wordpress-maintenance-plans/), [salesandmarketing.com](https://salesandmarketing.com/combatting-the-top-causes-of-agency-customer-churn/)).
