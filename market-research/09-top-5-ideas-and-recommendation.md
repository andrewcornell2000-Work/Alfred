# Top 5 Ideas & Final Recommendation

**The one-paragraph answer:** Build a **voice-to-report service for small security guard companies** — a guard sends a voice note at the end of a patrol or incident, and the security company's client receives a branded, timestamped, court-ready PDF report minutes later, with the original audio and transcript archived as evidence. No app for guards to install, no integration, flat monthly fee, $999 done-for-you setup. It scored highest across every research stream: the reports are contractually and legally mandated (nobody has to be convinced they need them), the exact price shape already exists in the market (Silvertrac charges $249/mo + $842–1,404 setup), the niche is unoccupied in AU/US, buyers are findable through public licensing registers and communities rather than cold calls, and the demo *is* the sales pitch — a prospect sends a voice note and gets their own branded report back. The same engine then extends to commercial cleaning, trades site-paperwork, and staff training as later verticals.

## 3. The Top 5, in detail

### #1 — Voice-to-Report for Small Security Companies ("court-ready reports from a voice note")

**What it is.** Guards at 5–30-guard security firms send a voice note via WhatsApp, SMS, or a QR-code browser recorder (no app install) after each patrol, shift, or incident. AI converts it into the firm's branded, timestamped Daily Activity Report or incident report PDF, delivered automatically to the firm's client (property manager, strata, event organiser) and archived with the original audio + transcript + guard confirmation as a court-ready evidence chain.

**Why it wins.**
- **Mandated demand**: DARs are a contractual deliverable and incident registers are statutory (kept 3 years, open to police inspection in AU). Nobody has to be convinced to need this — "your contracts already require this; here's a cheaper way to comply" is the easiest possible pitch for a non-salesperson.
- **Validated pricing**: Silvertrac charges $249/mo + a **mandatory $842–1,404 setup fee** on 12-month contracts. Your $999 setup + $149–299/mo flat undercuts it while being radically simpler. The fee is 0.3–1% of a small firm's revenue.
- **A structural moat matched to the niche**: guard turnover is 77–300% annually. Every app-based competitor must re-onboard its user base 1–3× a year. "Nothing to install, nothing to retrain" compounds in value with churn.
- **Whitespace confirmed by a dedicated validation dive** (report 07): incumbents' AI is text-polish inside their own apps; the voice-note→client-ready-PDF wedge is unoccupied in AU/US. Closest analogues (FieldOps in EU, GuardSync in AU strata) validate the concept without occupying the segment.
- **The demo is the sale**: publish a demo phone number — an owner sends a voice note about last night's shift and gets back their own branded PDF within minutes.

**Economics at scale (conservative):** 30 customers × $199/mo avg = ~$72k/yr recurring + setup fees, at 80%+ gross margin, serviceable solo. Serviceable market ~1,500–2,500 AU firms + 2,500–4,000 US firms.

**Risks (full table in report 07):** incumbents adding voice AI (window is quarters, not years — move fast); AI-report liability (mitigated by making the audio→transcript→draft→confirmation provenance chain the headline feature); WhatsApp-only reach (~49% AU / ~30% US — hence multi-channel capture from day one).

### #2 — NDIS Provider Audit-Evidence Stack ("your audit evidence writes itself")

**What it is.** For small registered NDIS providers (5–50 support workers): workers voice-note after shifts → compliant progress notes, auto-screened for incident triggers → incident register with 24-hour reportable-incident decision support and Commission notification drafts → training/complaints registers → one-click audit evidence packs mapped to the Practice Standards → quarterly mock-audit report.

**Why it's #2, not #1.** The tailwind is the biggest of anything researched — mandatory registration expands to all high-risk supports on **1 July 2027**, pushing thousands of unregistered providers into building audit evidence for the first time, and providers already pay consultants $2,500–$7,000 per audit-prep engagement. But the validation dive (report 08) found the "open field" premise is stale: Checkbase ($149–291/mo), FormaOS ($297/mo), Willow AI and Centro Assist all launched into this gap recently, and the sector demonstrably punishes outsiders — the cautionary tale is FormaOS, a strong solo-built compliance product whose case-study page still contains only "illustrative scenarios," not customers. Winning requires partnering with an NDIS compliance consultant for credibility and distribution (Willow is signing exactly those partners now), plus liability-hardening the 24-hour incident logic (seven-figure fines exist for late notification) and AU-region AI inference for participant privacy. **Bigger prize, harder and slower path, near-mandatory partner dependency.**

### #3 — AU Trades Site-Paperwork Pack ("your whole site file from a voice note")

**What it is.** For 1–20-person construction trades: describe the job by voice → site-specific SWMS + matching toolbox talk with QR sign-on sheet + daily site diary entries + written variation orders with signature links. Sold as a done-for-you compliance service: $999 setup (branded safety policy pack, templates configured) + $99–149/mo unlimited documents.

**Why it works.** This is pure grudge spend — principal contractors won't let a sub on site without a SWMS, NSW issues $3,600 on-the-spot fines, and SafeWork NSW says 60%+ of SWMS reviewed on site are inadequate. Sole traders already pay $29–97 *per document* repeatedly; HazardCo locks firms into 12-month contracts at AU$119–299/mo. Demand is query-driven ("SWMS generator" is actively searched), which suits inbound. The variations angle has built-in marketing: 83% of variation disputes with signed written approval resolve favourably vs 17% without.

**Why it's #3.** The SWMS niche already has 4+ AI generators (validating demand but crowding the obvious wedge), the $29 template anchors make $999 harder to justify for SWMS alone (the *bundle* is the answer), and monthly durability is weaker than security reporting. Jurisdiction-specific legal accuracy needs "review before use" framing.

### #4 — Commercial Cleaning Documentation Pack ("proof you were there, bids that win")

**What it is.** For commercial cleaning companies: crew photos + voice notes → branded completion/proof-of-service reports and monthly client reports; plus tender-pack assembly (insurance certs, WHS policy, sample SWMS, induction records) — the documents facility managers demand before awarding contracts.

**Why it works.** Cleaning is invisible-when-done-right, so "did you even come?" disputes are constant and contracts demand timestamped evidence; reporting is a documented *retention and bidding weapon* (Swept customers cite client reports as "a huge advantage when bidding new accounts"). 45,014 AU businesses, ~200% staff turnover (same no-app moat as security), reachable via ISSA/BSCAI and large owner Facebook groups.

**Why it's #4.** Thinner margins than security (labour is 50–90% of job cost), and the proof-of-service niche already has small app-first players (CrewProof, CleanProof, ProTeams, Swept). Best treated as **the second vertical for the #1 engine** once security has 10+ referenceable customers — same pipeline, different templates.

### #5 — Done-for-You Staff Training Portal (your original idea 1, repositioned)

**What it is.** The owner voice-notes or video-walks their procedures; you deliver a branded mobile training portal with lessons, checklists, quizzes, and completion records. $999–2,500 setup + $79–149/mo for hosting, updates, and new-hire tracking.

**Why it survives.** The market vacuum is real and documented: Trainual retreated upmarket (10-seat minimum, ~$249/mo, $1,000 mandatory implementation fee, "best for 25–1,000 employees") while free tiers serve nobody well, and 45% of small businesses have no written SOPs at all — which kills self-serve tools but is exactly what a done-for-you service solves. Turnover of 70–200% in cleaning/hospitality/security makes training recurring by nature.

**Why it's #5.** It's the weakest **standalone** offer: training software is the canonical shelfware category, the monthly fee protects static content (churn magnet), delivery is service-hours not software margin, and selling documentation to owners who've never written anything down is a consultative sale — the hardest kind for you. **Its right role is the cross-sell**: security and cleaning companies (ideas #1 and #4) have the industry's worst turnover, and once you host their reporting you're perfectly placed to host their training. As a cross-sell into an existing trusted relationship, the sales problem disappears.

## 4. Final recommendation

**Build idea #1 — the security voice-to-report service — with the architecture explicitly designed as a general "voice/photo in → branded compliance document out" engine**, so that cleaning (#4), trades paperwork (#3), and the training portal (#5) become template packs on the same codebase rather than new products. Hold NDIS (#2) as the documented expansion option to revisit only if a compliance-consultant partnership lands in your lap; it is the bigger prize but the wrong first move for a solo outsider.

Why this sequencing beats starting anywhere else:
1. **Security first is the easiest sale you'll ever have**: mandated deliverable, validated price shape, tiny cost relative to customer revenue, and a self-demonstrating product.
2. **One pipeline, four markets.** Whisper-class transcription → structured extraction → templated branded PDF → delivery + archive is identical across all top-4 ideas. The vertical is configuration, not code.
3. **The failure mode of each idea is covered by another.** If security proves slow to reach, the identical engine pivots to cleaning or trades with template changes, not a rebuild.

### Answering your distribution question directly ("is there a platform where people reach out to me?")

Yes — four, in priority order (full evidence in reports 06 and 07):

1. **Fiverr + Upwork Project Catalog** — the purest "buyers click buy" platforms. AI-service gigs at $999+ fixed scope are an established format (Fiverr Pro sellers list $1,500–$10,000 packages; Upwork's AI-services volume exceeds $300M/yr; top AI-gig sellers earn $3,000–10,000/mo). The 10–20% fee is your outsourced sales team. List the $999 security-report setup as a productised gig from day one.
2. **The demo phone number** — your unfair advantage as a builder. A security company owner sends a voice note and receives their own branded DAR back in minutes. No pitch, no call. Share the number in communities, outreach notes, and on every template download page.
3. **Template lead-magnet SEO** — free AU-specific DAR and incident-report templates (all existing template content is American). Every incumbent (Silvertrac, Belfry, THERMS) farms this keyword cluster, which is proof it converts. Buyers arrive pre-sold. Slow (3–9 months) but compounding and zero sales skill.
4. **White-label resellers** — once 5–10 customers exist, recruit 3–5 partners (security-industry insurance brokers, marketing agencies serving trades/security, bookkeepers) who keep 30–40% of the monthly fee for life and do all the selling. Documented precedents run $119k+/yr for solo fulfilment operations behind reseller fronts.

And the channels to consciously ignore: AppSumo (lifetime-deal economics are hostile to a fulfilment service — "lifetime deals are not revenue, they are debt"), Product Hunt (your buyers don't know it exists), Bark/Thumbtack (paid lead-chasing, the opposite of passive), trade-show booths ($18k+ and maximal face-to-face selling).

### Pricing (validated numbers, not guesses)

- **Setup: $999**, anchored against a $2,500 "premium" tier (3 months of monthly service bundled). Waive it for the first 3 AU customers in exchange for named case studies.
- **Monthly: $199–299 flat per company** (not per user — per-user pricing is the incumbents' structural weakness). $297/mo is the single most-validated local-SMB price point across all research.
- Month-to-month, 14-day money-back, and a monthly "value email" (reports generated, hours saved) — because SMBs reliably cancel services they can't see working, and ~43% of churn happens in the first 90 days.

### What the MVP needs to be (for the build phase)

1. **Capture:** WhatsApp Business API + Twilio SMS/MMS voice memo + a no-login QR/browser recorder (all three from day one — WhatsApp alone reaches only ~49% of Australians).
2. **Pipeline:** transcription (structure-don't-invent prompting) → extraction into the firm's report template → guard one-tap confirmation → branded PDF → auto-delivery to the client + archive.
3. **Evidence chain as the headline feature:** original audio + verbatim transcript + AI draft + confirmed final, all retained and exportable — "court-ready with the evidence attached."
4. **Owner dashboard:** report archive, client list, template settings, monthly value summary.
5. **Onboarding as a productised service:** a fixed-scope intake form (logo, report formats, client emails, guard roster) that you turn into a configured instance — the $999 deliverable, templated so each new customer is hours, not days.
