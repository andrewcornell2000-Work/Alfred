# Executive Summary Generator

**Source:** https://github.com/nimrodfisher/data-analytics-skills  
**Attribution:** nimrodfisher — data analytics skills collection.  
**Adapted for Alfred:** for condensing analysis into decision-ready outputs for Maersk finance leadership.

---

## When This Skill Applies

Use when:
- A detailed analysis needs to fit in 1–2 pages for a senior audience
- Preparing a board or leadership deck section from a longer workstream
- Someone says "just give me the headline" or "what's the bottom line?"
- A recurring report needs to lead with the key message rather than data tables
- A decision needs to be made quickly and the full report won't be read

---

## Six-Step Process

### Step 1 — Extract the top 3–5 insights
Go through the full analysis and keep only findings that **change or reinforce a decision**. Filter out interesting-but-not-actionable findings.

> If you have more than 5 insights, you haven't prioritised yet.

### Step 2 — Quantify every insight
Every single insight must carry a number:
- ✅ "Labour cost overspent by $420k (8%) against March budget, driven by APAC overtime"
- ❌ "Labour costs were significantly higher than expected"

Revenue at risk, cost saving, headcount affected, time to payback — pick one, put it in the sentence.

### Step 3 — Write the situation statement (one paragraph)
Explain:
- Why this analysis was done
- What question it answered
- Why the timing matters

One paragraph. No jargon. No acronyms without expansion.

### Step 4 — Apply the Pyramid Principle
Lead with the conclusion, then the evidence, then the detail.

```
❌ "We looked at 12 months of data across 6 regions and found that... [3 paragraphs later] ...costs are over budget."

✅ "APAC labour costs are 8% over budget. The primary driver is unplanned overtime 
   in March (+$420k). This is concentrated in Singapore operations (72% of the 
   overspend) following the ERP system cutover on March 4."
```

### Step 5 — State recommendations as specific actions
Each recommendation must answer:
- **What** — specific action, not a direction
- **Who** — named team or role responsible
- **Outcome** — expected measurable result
- **By when** — a date, not "soon"

### Step 6 — Write the decision block (if approval needed)
Final section explicitly states:
- The decision or approval required
- Investment or resource needed
- Expected return or outcome
- Deadline for the decision

---

## Output Format

```
## Situation
[One paragraph: context, question, timing]

## Key Findings
1. [Quantified finding — conclusion first]
2. [Quantified finding — conclusion first]
3. [Quantified finding — conclusion first]

## Recommendations
1. [Action] — [Owner] — [Expected outcome] — [By date]
2. [Action] — [Owner] — [Expected outcome] — [By date]

## Decision Required  ← only if approval needed
[Decision] | Investment: [X] | Expected return: [Y] | Deadline: [date]
```
